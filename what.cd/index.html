Gazelle release schedule - posted 1 day ago
Due to the overwhelming number of messages we've received wondering when people can get their hands on our shiny new codebase, we've decided to write up this quick overview of when the code will be publicly released.

Currently, the Gazelle codebase is, as far as we know, ready for the first release candidate. The only thing we're working on at the moment is documentation, which is proving to be long and tedious. We anticipate having this ready within the next two weeks, at which point Gazelle RC1 will be made available.

Gazelle RC1 will be accessible via controlled SVN access, granted to the any of following people who ask for it:

1. Admistrators of other trackers
2. VIPs on What.CD
3. People who are interested and truly motivated in starting up their own trackers. In order to qualify for this position, you must already have a domain name, server, staff, and 'business plan' that ensures that you won't become like one of the other thousand trackers that no one has ever heard of.

Once we've let RC1 run for a month or two without finding any serious bugs or exploits, we'll open up public SVN access, and anyone who wants can check out Gazelle.

Wild and untamed gazelles - posted 3 weeks ago
Two weeks ago marked a tragic day in the BitTorrent world - in a highly unexpected move, the Malaysian government ordered the shutdown of a number of sites hosted in Malaysian datacenters. Amongst the affected sites was the popular Mac-centered tracker, BrokenStones. As BrokenStones' server was quarantined, they had to regrettably announce their permanent closure.

Fortunately, as we have seen (and demonstrated) in the past, destroying a site is futile if the community and its ideals are allowed to remain. Although rebuilding a lost torrent site takes a monumental effort, the dedicated communities surrounding them are invariably capable and motivated enough to do so. For the past two weeks, a team of individuals has been working away at a replacement site, basing it off our currently unreleased Gazelle codebase.

After many sleepless nights and caffeine-filled days, the replacement site has been completed. It's been granted the name of Vortex Network, and is currently invitation only (power users can seek invites in the invite forum).

After having worked very closely with the new staff during the course of the entire process, we can say with full confidence that, with a little time and effort, they are fully capable of functioning as a more than fitting replacement for BrokenStones.

To all the displaced BrokenStones users - invites are available in many power user forums including our own, and on irc.vortexnetwork.org #vortexnetwork. We hope you have found your new home. To the Vortex team - we wish you the best of luck, and will continue to lend you our support whenever it is required.

--What.CD staff
